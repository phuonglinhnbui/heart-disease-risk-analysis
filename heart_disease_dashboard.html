Key Findings & Recommendations
  • Stroke history is the strongest single predictor of heart disease — patients with prior stroke have a 36.4% HD rate (4.9× the baseline). Early stroke recovery programs with cardiac monitoring are critical.
  • Physical inactivity doubles heart disease risk (13.8% vs 7.1%). Community health programs targeting sedentary adults — especially in the 55–69 age bracket — could have the greatest population-level impact.
  • Diabetic patients have 22% HD prevalence vs 6.5% in non-diabetic respondents. Integrated diabetes-cardiovascular care pathways and more frequent cardiac screening for diabetic patients are strongly indicated.
  • Kidney disease carries a 29.3% HD rate — nearly 4× the average. Cardiorenal syndrome is a well-established pathway; nephrologists and cardiologists should collaborate on screening protocols for at-risk patients.
  • Self-rated "Excellent" health has only 2.2% HD rate — confirming general health perception as a strong signal. This variable should be a top feature in any clinical risk scoring tool.
